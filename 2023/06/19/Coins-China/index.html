Coins-China

Post
发布时间 : 2023-06-19 21:19
阅读 :

Recently I bought a few commemorative coins as well as some commonly used coins - I haven’t touched a real coin for more than seven years, now people always pay online hhhh. The moment I got the coin, I felt back to the days when I was a child and tossed coins one by one in the piggy bank with anticipation…

Welcome to exchange commemorative coins! They can be sold directly, but I’ve never seen them used directly, because the price is usually higher than the denomination.

The following two coins are commemorative coins for the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics, with patterns of short track speed skating and alpine skiing respectively. I think it’s super pretty because it’s partly coloured!

  • 第24届冬季奥林匹克运动会 (24th Olympic Winter Games)
  • 中华人民共和国 2022 (People’s Republic of China)

The following two are about the Shanghai World Expo and the 70th anniversary of the victory of the Anti-Japanese War.

  • 中国2010年上海世博会 (Expo 2010 Shanghai China)
  • 中国人民抗日战争暨世界反法西斯战争胜利70周年 (70th Anniversary of the Victory of the Chinese People’s War of Resistance Against Japanese Aggression and the World Anti-Fascist War)

My family lives next to the Shanghai World Expo, which was built for the World Expo. Many countries built venues at that time to showcase their national characteristics. I remember that the British Pavilion is a huge plant seed preservation bank, while the Russian Pavilion is like a fairy tale world.

The Anti-Japanese War in China was one of the most important parts of World War II. It brought indelible grief and trauma to China, to every people. My grandfather(mother’s father)’s family was in Fushun, Liaoning, and the entire city was brutally slaughtered by the Japanese army! Not a single one alive… Forever wish for world peace!

The three coins below are part of the China World Natural and Cultural Heritage Series:

  • (the left one) 世界文化和自然遗产 - 黄山 (World Natural and Cultural Heritage - Mount Huangshan)
  • (the upper one) 世界文化和自然遗产 - 峨眉山 - 乐山大佛 (World Natural and Cultural Heritage - Emei Scenic Area - Leshan Giant Buddha)
  • (the right one) 世界文化和自然遗产 - 武夷山 (World Natural and Cultural Heritage - Mount Wuyi)

The following two are the most commonly used coins in China, and the patterns are chrysanthemum and lotus.
